Industry reports show that businesses who effectively leverage data outperform their competition but the journey to a data-driven culture is challenging.

According to the Economist Intelligence Unit, these businesses "are integrating data into their day-to-day operations. They are placing data at the heart of almost all important decisions. They are tolerant of question—even dissent—about business decisions being made, as long as the questioning is based on data and their analysis."

But moving to a data-driven culture is challenging. Executives may be reluctant to turn over decision-making to people who make decisions on the basis of data rather than expertise, concerns about privacy and security may limit data access, and business units may be reluctant to share data.

Even with systems and a supportive culture you need staff with the analytical skills required to take advantage of the data, and managers who know how to use the analysis to make effective decisions. Both skills could become scarce if reports are to be believed.

We understand that this is a very real challenge for businesses and it is something we have been researching. We are interested to meet and discuss with managers who are experiencing these difficulties and to help them work towards a more data-driven culture.
